# Hot and Sour Soup

<Tabs>
  <TabItem value="photos" label="Photos" default>
    A classic Chinese restaurant favorite that comes together in just 20 minutes. This hot and sour soup features silky egg ribbons, tender mushrooms, and tofu in a tangy, slightly spicy broth that's both comforting and flavorful.

    ![image](@site/docs/static/img/not-found.svg)
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem value="ingredients" label="Ingredients">
    ## Ingredients
    ```text title="Ingredients"
    8 cups chicken or vegetable broth
    8 oz shiitake mushrooms, thinly sliced, stems removed
    1 (8-oz) can bamboo shoots, drained (optional)
    1/4 cup rice vinegar
    1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
    2 teaspoons ground ginger
    1 teaspoon chili garlic sauce
    1/4 cup cornstarch
    2 large eggs, whisked
    8 oz firm tofu, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
    4 green onions, thinly sliced
    1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
    Kosher salt and white pepper (or black pepper) to taste
    ```

    <Admonition type="info" title="Meat Variation">
      <p>
        For a meat version, substitute 8 oz cooked pork (ground, shredded, or thinly sliced) for the tofu.
      </p>
    </Admonition>

    <Admonition type="warning" title="White Pepper">
      <p>
        White pepper has a stronger flavor than black pepper. Start with a small amount and adjust to preference.
      </p>
    </Admonition>

  </TabItem>

  <TabItem value="instructions" label="Instructions">

    1. Prepare the cornstarch slurry: Set aside 1/4 cup of **broth**. Whisk together this reserved **broth** and **cornstarch** until smooth.
    2. Add remaining 7 3/4 cups **broth**, **shiitake mushrooms**, **bamboo shoots** (if using), **rice vinegar**, **soy sauce**, **ground ginger**, and **chili garlic sauce** to a large stock pot. Heat over medium-high until simmering.
    3. Stir in the cornstarch mixture and cook for about 1 minute until thickened.
    4. While stirring the soup in circular motions, slowly drizzle the whisked **eggs** into the soup to form ribbons. Add **tofu**, half of the **green onions**, and **sesame oil**.
    5. Season with **salt** and a pinch of **white pepper** to taste. Adjust **vinegar** or **chili garlic sauce** as desired.
    6. Ladle immediately while hot, garnishing with the remaining **green onions**.

    <Admonition type="info" title="Timing">
      <p>
        Prep time: 5 minutes | Cook time: 15 minutes | Serves: 6-8
      </p>
    </Admonition>

  </TabItem>
